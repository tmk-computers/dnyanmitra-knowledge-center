# Writing Style and Mechanics

This document establishes the grammatical, spelling, numeric, and formatting rules required across all DASP Digital documentation.

---

## 🇬🇧 Spelling Rules (UK / Indian English)

DASP Digital platforms default to UK / Indian English spelling conventions. Do not use US spellings in external corporate or platform documents.

- **`-ise` vs. `-ize`**: Always write `-ise` (e.g., *digitise, customise, organise, categorise, standardise*).
- **`-our` vs. `-or`**: Always write `-our` (e.g., *colour, behaviour, favour*).
- **`-re` vs. `-er`**: Always write `-re` (e.g., *centre, theatre*).
- **Double Consonants**: Maintain double consonants on word inflections (e.g., *cancelled, travelling, modelling*).
- **Technical Exception**: CSS rules, programming variables, and API source code terms are written as defined by their compilers (e.g. `color` in CSS or `program` in IT code).

---

## 💵 Currency, Numbers & Dates

### 1. Currency Formatting
- Use the Indian Rupee symbol (**₹**) or **INR** prefix.
- Format large numbers using Lakhs and Crores rather than Millions/Billions for domestic Indian communications.
  - *Correct*: `₹2.5 Lakhs` or `INR 2,50,000`
  - *Incorrect*: `$3,000` or `INR 250,000` (Western comma spacing)

### 2. Date and Time Standards
- **Date Format**: Day-Month-Year (DD Month YYYY) with the month written out in full or short text.
  - *Correct*: `16 July 2026` or `16 Jul 2026`
  - *Incorrect*: `07/16/2026` (US format)
- **Time Format**: Write time in 12-hour AM/PM format or 24-hour format, always noting the timezone (IST - Indian Standard Time).
  - *Correct*: `11:30 AM IST` or `15:30 IST`

## 🗣️ Voice and Tone Guidelines

- **Active Voice**: Always prefer active voice for guides, SOPs, and user manuals.
  - *Passive*: "The document is reviewed by the clerk."
  - *Active*: "The clerk reviews the document."
- **Capitalization**: Use Sentence case for titles and headers (e.g. `## How to onboard your school`). Do not capitalize every word unless it is a proper noun (e.g. `DnyanMitra`, `CBSE`).
- **Oxford Comma**: Use the Oxford comma in lists containing three or more items.
  - *Example*: "Source student chairs, science supplies, and solar panels."
