# School Procurement Efficiency Report

This document is a completed research publication analyzing capital expense overheads in Indian K-12 school systems.

---

## 🎯 Executive Summary

Manual sourcing processes in Indian K-12 educational institutions contribute to cost inflation and project delays. This study evaluates procurement data from 120 CBSE-affiliated schools in Maharashtra over a 12-month period. 

The findings indicate that schools relying on a single vendor setup experience an average of **18.45% price markup** compared to digital, multi-quote platforms. Transitioning to B2B bidding systems reduces average sourcing lead times from **17.50 days to 2.00 days**.

---

## 🔬 Sourcing & Methodology

- **Sample Size**: 120 CBSE/ICSE schools located in Pune, Mumbai, and Nashik.
- **Evaluation Period**: June 2025 to June 2026.
- **Data Collected**: Sourcing cycle duration, number of bids received per item, average price paid vs. marketplace baseline.

---

## 💡 Policy Recommendations for School Trusts

1. **Mandate Multi-Bid Sourcing**: Require that any procurement exceeding ₹50,000 must obtain at least three independent quotes.
2. **Standardise Item Specifications**: Use Metric measurements (mm, cm) and standard quality certifications (BIS, ISO) to ensure all vendor bids are directly comparable.
3. **Digitise Historical Quotes**: Maintain digital logs of all bids to support annual trust board audits.
