This document contains a comprehensive index of **105 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)** structured across 21 core business categories. It serves as a transparent reference guide for prospective partners to review operational policy, payment frequencies, legal boundaries, and product details.

## 📂 Index of 105 Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Business Model
1. **Q1.1: What is the core business model of DASP Digital?**  
   - **A**: DASP Digital builds regional B2B marketplaces and cloud ERP systems. We appoint a Taluka Head to serve as the local technology implementation partner (Digital Transformation Centre) in each taluka.
2. **Q1.2: Is DnyanMitra a franchise or an agency model?**  
   - **A**: It is a B2B franchise partnership model where the Taluka Head holds exclusive operational rights for the allotted taluka.
3. **Q1.3: What is the Digital Transformation Centre (DTC)?**  
   - **A**: The DTC is the Taluka Head's local office and demo room where school administrators can physically inspect hardware and test software.
4. **Q1.4: How does DASP Digital ensure DTC profitability?**  
   - **A**: Profitability is driven by low administrative overheads and multiple recurring revenue streams (software subscriptions, hardware commissions, and AMCs).
5. **Q1.5: Does DASP Digital require a security deposit?**  
   - **A**: No, DASP Digital does not charge franchise fees or upfront security deposits. Partners invest in their own local office setup and FSE hiring.

### 2. Revenue & Earnings
6. **Q2.1: What are the primary sources of revenue for a Taluka Head?**  
   - **A**: Revenue comes from School/College ERP subscriptions, smart classroom hardware installations, B2B marketplace commissions, and Annual Maintenance Contracts (AMCs).
7. **Q2.2: Are there recurring revenue options?**  
   - **A**: Yes. ERP software subscriptions and AMC support packages are renewed annually, creating cumulative recurring revenue.
8. **Q2.3: Can a Taluka Head sell services to government schools?**  
   - **A**: Yes. While government schools have different procurement cycles, they buy smart board packages and digital library tools through public RFP systems on DnyanMitra.
9. **Q2.4: What is the average payback period for a DTC setup?**  
   - **A**: Based on pilot talukas, a well-managed DTC breaks even and recovers initial setup expenses within 4 to 6 months.
10. **Q2.5: Do we get commissions on sports facilities?**  
    - **A**: Yes, all sports bookings and equipment transactions generated via KridaMitra in your taluka yield commissions.

### 3. Commissions & Rates
11. **Q3.1: What is the commission rate for software ERP subscriptions?**  
    - **A**: The Taluka Head earns between 8% and 10% of the total annual ERP contract value.
12. **Q3.2: What is the commission rate for smart classroom hardware?**  
    - **A**: The commission is 3% to 4% of the total hardware billing value (excluding taxes).
13. **Q3.3: How are marketplace transaction commissions calculated?**  
    - **A**: We pay 1.5% to 2.5% on bulk marketplace sales (uniforms, shoes, textbooks, sports equipment).
14. **Q3.4: Who defines the platform margins?**  
    - **A**: Platform margins are set by DASP Digital HQ based on direct factory pricing contracts to keep prices competitive.
15. **Q3.5: Can a Taluka Head charge extra fees to client schools?**  
    - **A**: No. All charges must be officially invoiced through the DnyanMitra platform. Charging external fees violates the Code of Conduct.

### 4. Targets & Incentives
16. **Q4.1: Are there mandatory monthly sales targets?**  
    - **A**: Yes. Targets are structured quarterly (e.g. Month 1: 20 school surveys; Month 2: 10 demos; Month 3: first transaction closures).
17. **Q4.2: What happens if a Taluka Head misses a monthly target?**  
    - **A**: The District Head reviews the pipeline and offers field support. Consistent failure to meet targets over two quarters may lead to territory reviews.
18. **Q4.3: Are there bonuses for onboarding local vendors?**  
    - **A**: Yes. Onboarding 5 active local vendors in a quarter unlocks a flat ₹5,000 regional growth bonus.
19. **Q4.4: What is the Level 1 performance incentive milestone?**  
    - **A**: Generating ₹2 Lakh total contract sales in a month unlocks an extra 1% bonus commission on all hardware sales that month.
20. **Q4.5: Can incentive structures be modified individually?**  
    - **A**: No. Incentives are standardized across Maharashtra to maintain corporate compliance.

### 5. Training & Enablement
21. **Q5.1: Does DASP Digital provide initial training?**  
    - **A**: Yes. We run a mandatory 1-week training program covering CRM dashboard administration, product catalogs, and field presentation tactics.
22. **Q5.2: Is there training for the Field Sales Executive (FSE)?**  
    - **A**: Yes. The FSE receives field training, product guides, and scripts.
23. **Q5.3: Where is the training conducted?**  
    - **A**: Training is conducted online (Zoom/Google Meet) and supplemented by on-site visits from the District Head.
24. **Q5.4: Is there ongoing training for product updates?**  
    - **A**: Yes. Product updates and sales briefings are held monthly.
25. **Q5.5: Are training materials available offline?**  
    - **A**: Yes, all slides, videos, and guides are accessible via the partner portal.

### 6. CRM & Software Tools
26. **Q6.1: What CRM platform does DASP Digital use?**  
    - **A**: We use a custom-built CRM designed for school mapping and sales tracking.
27. **Q6.2: Can FSEs access the CRM from mobile devices?**  
    - **A**: Yes. The CRM portal is mobile-responsive and supports location tags for school visits.
28. **Q6.3: How are leads assigned in the CRM?**  
    - **A**: Leads are automatically mapped to the designated Taluka Head based on school geography.
29. **Q6.4: Does the CRM track client fee payment statuses?**  
    - **A**: Yes, the ERP module syncs with the CRM to display active subscriptions and pending renewals.
30. **Q6.5: Is there tech support if the CRM crashes?**  
    - **A**: Yes. DASP Digital provides a dedicated helpdesk for partners, active Monday to Saturday from 9 AM to 6 PM.

### 7. Products: Software ERP
31. **Q7.1: What is the pricing of the school ERP?**  
    - **A**: The standard ERP is priced between ₹150 and ₹250 per student per year, billed annually.
32. **Q7.2: Can the ERP work without internet connectivity?**  
    - **A**: Yes, the attendance and grading modules work offline and sync data when internet access is available.
33. **Q7.3: Is parent communication integrated?**  
    - **A**: Yes, automatic WhatsApp and SMS notifications are triggered for fee receipts, homework, and exam grades.
34. **Q7.4: Does the ERP support multiple schools under one trust?**  
    - **A**: Yes. The trust manager dashboard allows administrators to view reports across multiple campuses.
35. **Q7.5: Are student database backups automated?**  
    - **A**: Yes, databases are backed up automatically on secure AWS servers daily.

### 8. Products: Hardware
36. **Q8.1: What is included in the Smart Board package?**  
    - **A**: An interactive smart board screen, a short-throw projector, a wall mount, cabling, and an audio speaker column.
37. **Q8.2: What is the warranty period on hardware products?**  
    - **A**: All interactive smart boards carry a standard 3-year manufacturer warranty.
38. **Q8.3: Who handles hardware installation?**  
    - **A**: DASP Digital's certified local technician team handles installations, coordinated by the Taluka Head.
39. **Q8.4: Can biometric terminals integrate with school bus tracking?**  
    - **A**: Yes, RFID-based biometric terminals can link with school bus GPS trackers.
40. **Q8.5: What happens if a smart board screen breaks?**  
    - **A**: The school logs a ticket. If it's a manufacturing issue, the warranty covers it; physical damage is repaired on a paid basis.

### 9. Marketplace Sourcing
41. **Q9.1: What goods can schools buy on the DnyanMitra Marketplace?**  
    - **A**: Uniforms, PE kits, textbooks, science lab supplies, bulk stationery, and classroom furniture.
42. **Q9.2: How are marketplace prices kept competitive?**  
    - **A**: We bypass wholesalers and connect schools directly with verified manufacturers.
43. **Q9.3: What is the minimum order size for marketplace uniform custom prints?**  
    - **A**: The minimum order size is 100 sets per design.
44. **Q9.4: How are shipping and delivery managed?**  
    - **A**: Shipping is handled by pre-vetted national logistics providers, delivering directly to the school campus.
45. **Q9.5: Can a school return defective marketplace items?**  
    - **A**: Yes, defective items can be returned within 10 days of delivery, subject to our exchange policy.

### 10. Territory & Exclusivity
46. **Q10.1: Is territory exclusivity guaranteed?**  
    - **A**: Yes. Only one Taluka Head is appointed per taluka, and all sales in that area are credited to them.
47. **Q10.2: Can a Taluka Head execute sales in an adjacent taluka?**  
    - **A**: No. Out-of-territory sales are prohibited unless written approval is granted by the District Head.
48. **Q10.3: What happens if a school has campuses in multiple talukas?**  
    - **A**: The deal is handled by the Taluka Head where the purchasing office is located, or split as determined by the District Head.
49. **Q10.4: How are taluka boundaries defined?**  
    - **A**: Boundaries match official Maharashtra government revenue block limits.
50. **Q10.5: Can a partner manage two talukas simultaneously?**  
    - **A**: Only if the partner meets all targets in their primary taluka and receives District Head approval.

### 11. Tech Support & SLAs
51. **Q11.1: What is the SLA response time for software bugs?**  
    - **A**: Critical software bugs are resolved within 4 hours; general updates are processed within 24 hours.
52. **Q11.2: Who provides support to school administrators?**  
    - **A**: First-line support is managed by the Taluka Head DTC; complex queries are escalated to DASP Digital HQ.
53. **Q11.3: Is there weekend customer support?**  
    - **A**: Yes. Basic helpdesk support is available on Saturdays.
54. **Q11.4: How do schools submit technical support tickets?**  
    - **A**: Tickets are logged through the helpdesk portal or by contacting the Taluka Head's DTC office.
55. **Q11.5: Are replacement parts kept locally?**  
    - **A**: Basic replacement parts (cables, remote controls, stylus pens) are stocked at the Taluka DTC.

### 12. Marketing Assistance
56. **Q12.1: Does DASP Digital provide local marketing materials?**  
    - **A**: Yes. We provide digital templates for brochures, banners, product catalogs, and standees.
57. **Q12.2: Who pays for local advertising?**  
    - **A**: DASP Digital shares local marketing costs (e.g. newspaper inserts) on a 50:50 cost-share basis, with prior approval.
58. **Q12.3: Are school awareness events supported?**  
    - **A**: Yes. DASP Digital provides slide decks, product demo kits, and technical speakers for regional events.
59. **Q12.4: Can a Taluka Head run independent online advertising?**  
    - **A**: Yes, provided all ad copy matches DASP Digital brand guidelines.
60. **Q12.5: Do we get business cards?**  
    - **A**: Yes, branded digital business card formats are provided to all active partners upon onboarding.

### 13. Competition & Positioning
61. **Q13.1: How does DnyanMitra compare to generic ERP software?**  
    - **A**: DnyanMitra combines ERP software with a physical marketplace and local DTC support, which standalone software companies cannot replicate.
62. **Q13.2: How do we counter cheaper standalone software offerings?**  
    - **A**: Emphasize our localized physical support, parent alert integrations, and lack of hidden maintenance costs.
63. **Q13.3: What is our positioning statement?**  
    - **A**: We position ourselves as local digital transformation partners, not just software vendors.
64. **Q13.4: How do we address school security concerns?**  
    - **A**: Highlighting DASP Digital's secure cloud database, data encryption protocols, and user role limitations.
65. **Q13.5: What are the main competitors in rural Maharashtra?**  
    - **A**: Competitors are typically fragmented local IT shops and outdated offline administration methods.

### 14. Vendor Onboarding & KYC
66. **Q14.1: Who is eligible to register as a vendor?**  
    - **A**: Verified manufacturers, distributors, uniform printers, and local maintenance contractors.
67. **Q14.2: What documents are required for vendor registration?**  
    - **A**: PAN Card, GSTIN registration certificate, active bank account details, and proof of physical business address.
68. **Q14.3: Is a physical site visit required for vendor onboarding?**  
    - **A**: Yes. The Taluka Head must physically inspect the vendor's premises before approving their registration.
69. **Q14.4: How long does the vendor approval process take?**  
    - **A**: Approvals are processed within 3 business days once the site visit report is submitted.
70. **Q14.5: Can a vendor be delisted?**  
    - **A**: Yes, vendors who deliver defective goods or violate pricing rules are delisted.

### 15. School Visits & Audits
71. **Q15.1: How many school visits should the FSE complete daily?**  
    - **A**: The FSE is expected to complete 3 to 4 on-site school visits daily.
72. **Q15.2: What is the goal of the first school visit?**  
    - **A**: To map the school's size, introduce DnyanMitra, and build initial rapport.
73. **Q15.3: How do we conduct a digital audit?**  
    - **A**: We use the DASP CRM audit template to inventory smart boards, computer systems, and administrative processes.
74. **Q15.4: Are audits free for the school?**  
    - **A**: Yes, the initial audit report is free and serves as a tool to open sales conversations.
75. **Q15.5: Who presents the final audit report?**  
    - **A**: The Taluka Head presents the report to the school principal or trust directors.

### 16. Sales Negotiations & Proposals
76. **Q16.1: Who drafts the product proposals?**  
    - **A**: The Taluka Head drafts proposals using templates in the DASP CRM.
77. **Q16.2: Can a Taluka Head offer custom discounts?**  
    - **A**: Only with written approval from the District Head.
78. **Q16.3: What is the contract duration for software licensing?**  
    - **A**: Standard contracts are signed for 12 months, with auto-renewal options.
79. **Q16.4: How are smart board package payments structured?**  
    - **A**: Typically 50% advance upon order placement, and 50% post-installation sign-off.
80. **Q16.5: How do we handle trustee board reviews?**  
    - **A**: We present custom-tailored ROI summaries showing administrative time and cost savings.

### 17. Platform Technology & Security
81. **Q17.1: Where are DnyanMitra cloud servers hosted?**  
    - **A**: On secure AWS servers based in Mumbai, India.
82. **Q17.2: Is student data shared with third parties?**  
    - **A**: No, student data is encrypted and remains strictly confidential.
83. **Q17.3: Does the ERP support automatic data recovery?**  
    - **A**: Yes, automated data backups are processed daily.
84. **Q17.4: What happens during server downtime?**  
    - **A**: Offline-first clients cache logs locally and upload changes when servers reconnect.
85. **Q17.5: Is the software compatible with older computers?**  
    - **A**: Yes, the ERP is web-based and runs on basic browsers.

### 18. Future Growth & Platforms
86. **Q18.1: Can a Taluka Head represent KridaMitra?**  
    - **A**: Yes, the Taluka Head represents both DnyanMitra and KridaMitra.
87. **Q18.2: When will KrushiMitra launch?**  
    - **A**: KrushiMitra (agricultural marketplace) is planned for Phase 2 expansion.
88. **Q18.3: What is the ZilaValley platform?**  
    - **A**: Our future platform for local municipal procurement.
89. **Q18.4: Will we have access to future platforms?**  
    - **A**: Yes, active Taluka Heads will have first right of refusal for regional roles on new DASP platforms.
90. **Q18.5: Does DASP Digital plan to expand outside Maharashtra?**  
    - **A**: Yes, interstate expansion is planned for Year 4.

### 19. Legal Agreements & Compliance
91. **Q19.1: What is the term of the partnership agreement?**  
    - **A**: The initial agreement is signed for 12 months and is renewable annually based on performance.
92. **Q19.2: Can either party terminate the agreement early?**  
    - **A**: Yes, with a 30-day written notice, or immediately in case of breach of conduct.
93. **Q19.3: Who owns the customer relationship records?**  
    - **A**: All school leads, data logs, and transaction records are owned by DASP Digital.
94. **Q19.4: Is there a non-compete clause?**  
    - **A**: Yes, partners cannot represent competing EdTech or B2B platforms during the contract term.
95. **Q19.5: How are local contract disputes resolved?**  
    - **A**: Disputes are referred to the District Head and escalations are subject to Pune jurisdiction.

### 20. Payments, Payouts & Settlement
96. **Q20.1: What is the payout cycle for commissions?**  
    - **A**: Commissions are calculated monthly and paid by the 10th of the following month.
97. **Q20.2: How are payments disbursed?**  
    - **A**: Directly into the partner's verified bank account.
98. **Q20.3: Is tax deducted at source (TDS)?**  
    - **A**: Yes, TDS is deducted as per government guidelines.
99. **Q20.4: Do we get detailed monthly commission statements?**  
    - **A**: Yes, itemized statements are published in the partner CRM dashboard.
100. **Q20.5: How are customer refund disputes settled?**  
     - **A**: Handled by DASP billing teams; commissions on refunded items are adjusted in the next cycle.

### 21. Performance Review & Termination
101. **Q21.1: How often are Taluka Head reviews conducted?**  
     - **A**: Reviews are conducted weekly (progress checks), monthly (financial targets), and quarterly (business planning).
102. **Q21.2: What are the grounds for immediate termination?**  
     - **A**: Code of conduct violations, requesting kickbacks, falsifying vendor sites, or data leaks.
103. **Q21.3: What is the performance improvement plan (PIP)?**  
     - **A**: A 30-day plan with clear goals provided to underperforming partners before contract reviews.
104. **Q21.4: How are FSE performance issues handled?**  
     - **A**: The Taluka Head manages FSE performance issues, with support from the District Head.
105. **Q21.5: What happens to active clients if a partnership is terminated?**  
     - **A**: Clients are transferred to the District Head until a new Taluka Head is appointed.
