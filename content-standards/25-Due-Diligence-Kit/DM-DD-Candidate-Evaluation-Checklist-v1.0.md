## 🏛️ Executive Summary

To build a professional DTC network, DASP Digital relies on objective vetting rather than personal intuition. District Heads must score each candidate using this standard matrix following the second meeting (Taluka Business Planning Workshop).

---

## 📊 1. Candidate Scorecard Rubric

District Heads must score candidates from 1 to 5 on each metric (1 = Poor, 5 = Excellent) and apply the weights below:

| Assessment Area | Weight | Core Competency Checked |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Communication Skills** | 20% | Ability to explain digital concepts to school boards with a professional tone. |
| **Local Education Network** | 20% | Direct personal access to regional school management trustees and principals. |
| **Leadership & Hiring** | 15% | Experience managing teams and capacity to recruit and guide FSE personnel. |
| **Business & ROI Mindset**| 15% | Understanding of investment payback periods, operational margins, and cost controls. |
| **Technology Understanding**| 10% | Familiarity with cloud dashboards, ERP applications, and smart boards. |
| **Time Availability** | 10% | Capacity to dedicate at least 25 hours per week to DTC activities. |
| **Professional Reputation**| 10% | Standing in the local community, positive references, and clean credit history. |

---

## 📈 2. Overall Scoring Thresholds

After multiplying each score by its weight, calculate the final percentage:

```text
Final Score (%) = Sum of (Score * Weight) * 20
```

- **Score 85% to 100% (Category A - Highly Approved)**:
  - Proceed immediately to Partnership Agreement signing.
- **Score 70% to 84% (Category B - Conditional Onboarding)**:
  - Proceed to final interview with Division Head; requires 2 weeks of closely monitored training.
- **Score Below 70% (Category C - Rejected)**:
  - Candidate is not a fit. Archive application and send standard rejection letter.
